Baltimore Alcohol Outlets

For a reporting project, I've been looking at alcohol outlets in Baltimore.

The city sent me their most recent "master list" of the businesses licensed to sell alcohol, including their name and address. See file: 240520_Baltimore_master.csv

I geocoded the data, looked at values that didn't geocode properly, and integrated cleaning code and ultimately some manual changes to get them all to generate latitude and longitude. THIS TOOK A LOT OF EFFORT! See Jupyter notebook: Baltimore alcohol cleaning and geocoding

Then with a big boost to start from TA Thomas, I identified outlets with certain license-types (LA, LA2, WA) that are not allowed to be in residential areas, but appear to be thus located. See Jupyter notebook: identifying zoning

I output a list of these irregular outlets to check with the city regulators. And I moved the data — first from Python to CSV, then to GeoJSON, then to Mapbox — into a map that shows the residentially zoned areas (light green), all the alcohol outlets, those the Baltimore City government concedes are out of conformance with zoning law (red outline), and the couple dozen outlets that appear to be based on my analysis (orange outline).
